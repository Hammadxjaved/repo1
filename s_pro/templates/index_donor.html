In India, the eligibility criteria for blood donation are set by the National Blood Transfusion Council (NBTC) and the Central Drugs Standard Control Organization (CDSCO). Some of the specific criteria for donor eligibility in India include:

Age: Donors must be between the ages of 18 and 65 years old.
Weight: Donors must weigh at least 50 kilograms or 110 pounds.
Hemoglobin levels: Donors must have a minimum hemoglobin level of 12.5 grams per deciliter.
Health conditions: Donors must be in good health and free from any infectious diseases, such as HIV, hepatitis B or C, syphilis, or malaria.
Medications: Donors who are taking certain medications, such as blood thinners, may be ineligible to donate blood.
Pregnancy: Pregnant women are not eligible to donate blood, and there may be a waiting period after giving birth before they can donate.
Recent surgeries or illnesses: Donors who have had surgery or a serious illness in the past few months may be ineligible to donate blood.
Travel history: Donors who have traveled to certain countries or regions may be ineligible to donate blood due to the risk of infectious diseases.

It's important to note that these eligibility criteria may vary depending on the specific blood donation organization or blood bank. It's always a good idea to check with your local blood donation center for the most up-to-date information on donor eligibility.
