World Blood Donor Day

World Blood Donor Day is an international day observed on June 14th every year to raise awareness about the need for safe blood and to thank voluntary, unpaid blood donors for their life-saving gifts of blood.
Primary objective is to encourage more people to donate blood regularly and to highlight the importance of voluntary blood donation in saving lives. The day is celebrated in more than 170 countries worldwide, and it is organized by the World Health Organization (WHO) in collaboration with national health authorities, blood transfusion services, and other organizations.
Some of the events and activities that are typically organized on World Blood Donor Day include blood donation drives, educational and awareness-raising campaigns, award ceremonies to recognize outstanding blood donors and advocates, and media events to promote the importance of blood donation.
